Score

Your overall performance number — a weighted average across every question you've attempted, factoring in correctness. Not shown until you've attempted at least one question in that subject/track/topic.

Accuracy

The percentage of attempted questions you answered correctly. Score and Accuracy answer different questions: Score also weighs how much you've attempted, Accuracy only cares about your hit rate on what you actually tried.

Coverage / Journey Completed

How much of the available content you've engaged with — topics attempted at least once, tracks and subjects proportionally. This tracks breadth, not correctness, so it's normal to have high coverage with a modest score, or the reverse.

Mastery Level

Beginner, Intermediate, Advanced, or Expert — a stage reflecting how far you've progressed, not a grade like Score. It only advances with consistent, demonstrated performance, so a "Beginner" tag just means you're early on, not that you did poorly.

Easy / Medium / Hard

A breakdown of your attempted/correct counts by question difficulty, shown only for tiers you've actually attempted — useful for seeing whether you're stronger on fundamentals or advanced material.

Mandatory / Recommended / Optional

How subjects are prioritized for this job role. Mandatory subjects are core to the role, Recommended subjects round out your skills, and Optional subjects are supplementary — a good default order to work through if you're not sure where to start.
