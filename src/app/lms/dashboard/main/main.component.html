@if (activeTooltip === 'questions') {

Overview

Your question bank is the foundation of all assessments on the platform. Questions you create go through a review cycle before going live to learners.

Metrics explained

Live (Approved)

Questions reviewed, approved, and actively serving learners in quizzes.

Pending

Submitted but awaiting admin review. These are not visible to learners yet.

Trivia

Multiple-choice factual questions. Best for quick knowledge checks and gamified learning.

Interview

Open-ended questions for deeper evaluation, used in interview prep tracks.

Pro tips

  • → Aim for an approval rate above 70% — it signals well-scoped, accurate questions.
  • → Balance Trivia and Interview questions to serve both quick learners and serious candidates.
  • → Review pending questions regularly — a large backlog slows your content going live.
} @if (activeTooltip === 'lessons') {

Overview

Lessons are structured learning units assigned to topics. Learners work through them in sequence to build knowledge before attempting assessments.

Metrics explained

Completed / Created

Lessons fully written and published vs. total lessons started. Higher is better.

Views

Total times learners have opened and read your lessons.

Pending

Lessons drafted but not yet submitted for review or completion.

Pro tips

  • → Keep each lesson focused on a single concept — shorter lessons have higher completion rates.
  • → Add lessons before quizzes for any topic — learners perform better with structured pre-reading.
  • → High view counts with low completion suggest the lesson may be too long or complex.
} @if (activeTooltip === 'quizzes') {

Overview

Quizzes are timed assessments built from your approved questions. Once published, learners can attempt them and their scores are tracked automatically.

How it works

1

You need approved questions before creating a quiz.

2

Select questions, set a time limit, passing score, and number of attempts allowed.

3

Publish the quiz — it becomes live immediately for learners enrolled in your subject.

4

Track attempt counts, accuracy rates, and pass rates from your dashboard.

Pro tips

  • → Create topic-specific quizzes rather than one large quiz — learners retain more with focused assessments.
  • → Mix Trivia and Interview questions to assess both recall and deeper understanding.
} @if (activeTooltip === 'topics') {

Overview

Topics are the building blocks of your subject. They organise your questions, lessons, and quizzes into coherent sections that learners navigate through.

Metrics explained

Active

Topics visible to learners with at least one piece of published content attached.

Pending

Topics created but not yet activated — no content attached or awaiting admin approval.

Active coverage %

The ratio of active topics to total. Higher coverage means more of your subject is accessible to learners.

Pro tips

  • → Create topics before adding content — questions and lessons are linked to topics.
  • → Aim for 100% active coverage so learners have no gaps in your subject map.
  • → Keep topic names short and clear — learners use them to navigate your subject.
